#### **Grazing all day**
Eat 2–3 solid meals, ideally within a 10-hour window. Let your cells breathe.

You can think of your metabolism like a **breathing pattern**:
- **Feeding = inhale** → cells take in nutrients, store energy, build.
- **Fasting = exhale** → cells release stored energy, clean up, repair.
#### **Cardio is good for heart health, but strength training adds muscle**
* Muscle is the metabolic currency.
* Metabolism is the **sum total** of all the energy transactions in your body. it’s a dynamic system where your body:
	1. **Extracts energy** from food (catabolism).
	2. **Uses energy** to build, repair, and maintain cells (anabolism).
	3. **Balances** how much is used now vs. stored for later. 
* You eat → energy gets extracted → it’s either **spent immediately** or **stored** (multiple storage layers and **fat stores energy**).
- All cells use that energy to **maintain life and structure**.
- Muscles, **being energy-hungry** to maintain their structure, burn more calories, both during exercise and at rest.
- When intake < demand → the body taps fat for energy → you lose weight. 

**Walking/movement** is the single most easy thing to keep you healthy. Post-meal walks control glucose spikes.

**Eat healthy fats**. Low-fat alternatives are processed. **Quality over quantity.**

#### **Comfort vs. resilience: Your daily choices matter.**  
Comfort habits offer short-term relief but often erode long-term strength. Resilience-building habits may feel harder at first, but they grow **emotional stamina, clarity, and self-respect over time.**
