Most people **avoid fundamentals** because they don't have the **guts** to become great at them. 

*When you eliminate all that is **unnecessary**, you are left with just the basics and whether or not you have mastered them.*

We waste too much time debating **edge cases**. Edge cases are what-ifs, the minor details - things that might make a 2% difference, but distract you from the real work that makes 80% difference.

***The greatest skill in any endeavor is doing the work.***

It's easier to tell people you are working on a new project, doing more research, but hard to say that you are focusing on the basics -- **we try to focus on adding more details onto something, but hardly try to find issues that could be rooted in the basics**.

If you don't have a habit a brushing every night, that shiny new flossing device isn't gonna make a difference. **Without the fundamentals, details are useless!!**
